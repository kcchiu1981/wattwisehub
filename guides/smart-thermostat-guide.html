How to choose a smart thermostat

Pick a thermostat that supports your HVAC system and your real routine. Look for:

  • Compatibility with your HVAC system (heat pump, furnace, etc.)
  • Scheduling that matches how your home is used
  • Reports (runtime/usage insights) so you can verify improvement
  • Room sensors if comfort varies by room
Practical rule: The best thermostat is the one you’ll actually configure (and keep configured).

Install checklist

Before you start, take a photo of your existing wiring. Label wires if needed, and confirm you have a stable Wi-Fi signal where the thermostat sits.

  • Turn off HVAC power at the breaker
  • Photograph the wiring (don’t rely on memory)
  • Verify system compatibility (especially for heat pumps)
  • Complete setup in the app and test heat/cool

Best settings: schedules & eco modes

Most savings come from reducing unnecessary runtime. Your schedule should reflect when people are actually home and awake.

  • Use modest setbacks (avoid extreme swings)
  • Adjust gradually over a week, not all at once
  • Use “away” / occupancy features if reliable

Common mistakes (and fixes)

Mistake #1: Leaving default schedules

Default schedules rarely match your life. Set wake/sleep/away times you actually follow.

Mistake #2: Ignoring drafts and comfort issues

If your home leaks air, your thermostat will run longer no matter how smart it is. Seal drafts first—especially door bottom gaps.

Mistake #3: Dirty filters and poor airflow

A clogged HVAC filter restricts airflow and can increase runtime. Replace filters on a schedule.

FAQ

Do smart thermostats really save money?

They can—if you set schedules that match your routine and reduce drafts/airflow issues that force long runtime.

What’s the most common smart thermostat mistake?

Leaving default schedules, using extreme temperature swings, and ignoring drafts or dirty filters.

Do I need room sensors?

If your home has uneven comfort across rooms, sensors often improve comfort and reduce over-conditioning.
