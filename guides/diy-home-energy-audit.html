What you need (optional but helpful)

  • Last 2–3 utility bills (or your utility portal)
  • A phone camera (to capture model numbers + problem spots)
  • If you have it: a plug-in energy meter or a smart plug with usage tracking

Step 1 — Establish a baseline (10 minutes)

  • Write down your typical monthly kWh and total bill.
  • Note your rate type: flat, tiered, or time-of-use (TOU).
  • Circle seasonal spikes (summer cooling / winter heating).

Why this matters: without a baseline, you can’t prove savings. After changes, compare the next bill (or the next 2–4 weeks of kWh) against your baseline.

Step 2 — Quick walkthrough audit (30 minutes)

A) Air leaks & drafts (highest ROI)

  • Check doors/windows for visible gaps and cold/hot drafts.
  • Look at attic hatches, recessed lights, and cable/pipe penetrations.
  • Look for “dust streaks” near vents or baseboards (a sign of air movement).

Most homes waste a surprising amount of HVAC energy through leakage. Fixing leaks often pays back faster than buying new gadgets.

B) HVAC basics

  • Replace or clean HVAC filter (if applicable).
  • Make sure supply/return vents aren’t blocked by furniture.
  • Check thermostat schedule (many are set to “always comfortable” = always expensive).

Next: use a smart thermostat only if you will actually configure schedules and occupancy.

C) Lighting & “always-on” devices

  • Identify remaining incandescent/halogen bulbs; plan to convert to LED.
  • Find always-on loads: old set-top boxes, gaming consoles, desktop PCs, spare fridges/freezers.
  • Use a smart power strip for entertainment centers.
Fast win: Many bills hide in standby power (TV boxes, consoles, PCs). TrickleStar Advanced PowerStrip (control outlet + switched outlets) can cut “always-on” waste →

D) Hot water & laundry

  • Set water heater temp to ~49°C / 120°F (unless your situation requires higher).
  • Wash clothes cold when possible; line-dry light loads when practical.

Step 3 — Prioritize fixes (the “Top 3” rule)

Pick the top 3 actions that are low effort + high impact. Typical winners:

  1. Seal drafts (weatherstripping, door sweeps)
  2. Thermostat optimization (schedule + setbacks)
  3. LED conversion + cut standby loads

Quick verification plan (don’t skip)

  • After changes, compare your next bill’s kWh vs baseline.
  • If you have TOU rates, compare peak-hour usage before/after.
  • Document what you changed + date.

Simple rule: If you can’t measure it, you can’t improve it reliably.

FAQ

Do I need a professional audit?

Not at first. A DIY audit finds the majority of “easy savings.” Consider a pro audit later if you suspect major insulation/duct issues or want blower-door testing.

What’s the fastest savings?

Air sealing + thermostat schedule + reducing standby loads usually wins for speed and cost.
